Title: Percolating Human Computer Interaction

Year: 2024

Medium: GE Coffeematic Coffee Maker 10 Cup, ASUS M2NPV-VM Motherboard, 8GB DDR2 RAM, Linux Mint OS, HIS HD 4670 IceQ Graphics Card, Starbucks Pike Place blend

Dimension: 2 x 1.5 x 1.5 feet

Percolating HCI is an ongoing project featuring a coffee gadget interfaced with DDR3 unbuffered DIMMS.

Coffeematic PC examines flows from perspectives of percolation and design - extending headroom within the operating environment while percolating the melodies encountered through its configuration.

Coffeematic PC percolates Java like a regular coffee maker. Very hot Java.

A pump takes the caffenated slurry (~90C/194F) and circulates it thru two radiators sitting atop Coffeematic PC's crown -> down to a central processing unit (CPU) tucked within an ASUS M2NPV-VM motherboard snugly strapped to Coffeematic PC's athletic back.

Java continues through an artery returning to Coffeematic PC's reproduced caraffe.

The process repeats until Java is integrated with end-user or the machine is powered off.
